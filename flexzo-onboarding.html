Block 01

ICP & Business Context

The exact language buyers use when they feel their problem is what AI models index and what Google ranks. We need this before writing a single word.

Q1
Who is your primary buyer at an NHS Trust? Job title and department.
Head of Nursing? Director of Workforce? Operations lead? This determines the search terms and content angles — clinical language vs. operational language are completely different.
Q2
What's the single pain that closed your last 3 deals?
Not the feature they liked — the breaking point. Was it locum agency costs? Bank fill rate? Compliance burden? Shift fulfilment failure? That pain goes into the first 10 articles.
Q3
Which NHS Trusts can we name publicly as anchor clients?
Named case studies are the single fastest way to build AI citation authority in the NHS space. Even a one-paragraph win story with a named Trust outperforms 10 generic blog posts.
Q4
What objection kills deals most often?
Integration complexity? Procurement process? Comparison with Patchwork or Lantum? Objection-handling content ranks extremely well for bottom-of-funnel searches and AI citations.
Q5
What's the typical sales cycle and deal size for a mid-size NHS Trust?
Helps us calibrate content depth. Long cycles need thought leadership and trust-building content. Short cycles need high-intent comparison and feature pages.

Block 02

US Expansion Goals

The US site has 5 pages vs. 50+ for UK and zero live jobs. We need to know where to focus effort and how fast US needs to move.

Q6
Which US states or cities are you targeting first?
Texas, California, and Florida dominate US healthcare staffing volume. But your buyer and angle differ by state. We need to know where to build the first /us/jobs and landing pages.
Q7
Is the US ICP the same as the UK — or a different buyer persona?
NHS Trusts are a UK construct. In the US, the buyer is typically a hospital group, staffing agency, or health system. If the ICP differs, the content architecture needs to be built from scratch — not translated from UK pages.
Q8
Do you have any US clients or live jobs yet?
Right now the US section has 0 live jobs and 13 ghost URLs being crawled daily. We need to know if that changes soon or if we build entirely fresh. This affects the sitemap split and crawl priority immediately.
Q9
Is US urgency as high as UK, or is UK the priority for the next 90 days?
We can run both in parallel but that splits bandwidth. A clear priority steer helps us build the right technical architecture and allocate content output where it drives fastest returns.
Q10
Who are the US competitors you're watching most closely?
CareRev and Shiftmed are in the proposal. Are there others? Competitor comparison pages and alternative landing pages are the fastest route to AI citations in a new market.

Block 04

Content & Sales Intelligence

The raw material for everything we write. Sales recordings are worth more than any brief — they contain exact buyer language, verbatim.

Q17
Can you give us access to 3–5 sales call recordings?
Fireflies, Gong, Zoom, Loom — any format. We're listening for the exact phrases NHS buyers use to describe their problem. That language goes directly into article titles, meta descriptions, and hero copy.
Q18
Who approves content before it goes live — and what's the SLA?
We write fast. We need a named reviewer and a target turnaround (24h? 48h?). Slow approval cycles are the #1 reason content programmes stall in month one.
Q19
Are there any topics, competitor names, or claims we can't touch?
Legal, compliance, or commercial restrictions on what we can publish. Better to know now than after drafting. NHS regulatory language in particular sometimes needs careful framing.
Q20
Is there any unpublished content — case studies, whitepapers, internal guides?
The fastest wins in month one often come from rescuing content that already exists but never got indexed. Even an internal onboarding doc can become a high-value article with light editing.
Q21
Are there NHS Trust contacts willing to co-author or be quoted in content?
A quote from a Head of Nursing at a named Trust in an article instantly signals authority to AI models. Even one willing contact is worth 20 anonymous articles.

How AI Peekaboo Works for Flexzo

It runs in the background, 24/7. Every time an AI model responds to a tracked prompt, we log whether Flexzo was cited — and who got cited instead.

30 prompts tracked, every day
We fire every tracked prompt at ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Grok on a daily basis. Flexzo's mention rate is logged each time. You see momentum in real time, not just monthly.
Content ROI is measurable
When a new article goes live, we can see within days whether it shifts a prompt's response. That closes the loop between content production and AI citation — no guessing, no lag.
Competitor intelligence, always on
Every time Patchwork, Lantum, CareRev, or Shiftmed gets cited instead of Flexzo, we log it. The gap is the brief. We always know exactly what to write next.
